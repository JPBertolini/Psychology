# Cialdini — 7 principles

Source: [Influence](../../reference/Cialdini-Roberto/Influence.md) — *The Psychology of Persuasion*.

Cialdini groups thousands of compliance tactics into six core principles plus an introductory chapter on automatic influence. One-line summary per chapter. Deep dives go in `factors/` when ready.

| Ch | Principle | Slug | Summary |
|----|-----------|------|---------|
| 1 | Weapons of Influence | `weapons-of-influence` | Automatic compliance via fixed-action patterns; the "click, whirr" of mindless yes. |
| 2 | Reciprocation | `reciprocation` | Obligation to repay what another person has provided — gifts, favors, concessions. |
| 3 | Commitment and Consistency | `commitment-consistency` | Align with prior commitments; small initial yes escalates to larger ones. |
| 4 | Social Proof | `social-proof` | Look to others' behavior to decide correct action, especially under uncertainty. |
| 5 | Liking | `liking` | Say yes more readily to people we know and like — similarity, praise, cooperation. |
| 6 | Authority | `authority` | Defer to credible experts and symbols of authority. |
| 7 | Scarcity | `scarcity` | Value rises when availability is limited — loss aversion and competition for scarce goods. |
