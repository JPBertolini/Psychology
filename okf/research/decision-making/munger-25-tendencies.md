# Munger — 25 tendencies

Source: [Poor Charlie's Almanack](../../reference/Munger-Charles/Poor%20Charlie%E2%80%99s%20Almanack.md) — *The Psychology of Human Misjudgment* (Talk Eleven).

One-line summary per tendency. Deep dives go in `factors/` when ready.

| # | Tendency | Slug | Summary |
|---|----------|------|---------|
| 1 | Reward and Punishment Super Response | `reward-punishment` | Incentives and disincentives reshape cognition and behavior more powerfully than people recognize. |
| 2 | Liking/Loving | `liking-loving` | Favor people and things we like; overlook faults and comply with their wishes. |
| 3 | Disliking/Hating | `disliking-hating` | Distort judgment against disliked people or things; ignore their virtues. |
| 4 | Doubt-Avoidance | `doubt-avoidance` | Rush to decision to escape the discomfort of uncertainty. |
| 5 | Inconsistency-Avoidance | `inconsistency-avoidance` | Resist changing mind once committed; consistency locks in prior positions. |
| 6 | Curiosity | `curiosity` | Drive to explore and learn; can be exploited to hold attention. |
| 7 | Kantian Fairness | `kantian-fairness` | Expect and enforce reciprocal fairness in exchanges. |
| 8 | Envy/Jealousy | `envy-jealousy` | Powerful destructive drive; Munger argues it rivals or exceeds greed. |
| 9 | Reciprocation | `reciprocation` | Strong urge to repay favors, concessions, and gifts in kind. |
| 10 | Influence-from-Mere-Association | `mere-association` | Pavlovian linking; judge quality by associated context (ads, price signals). |
| 11 | Simple, Pain-Avoiding Psychological Denial | `psychological-denial` | Distort or deny reality to avoid psychological pain. |
| 12 | Excessive Self-Regard | `excessive-self-regard` | Overestimate own abilities, decisions, and possessions (endowment effect). |
| 13 | Overoptimism | `overoptimism` | Systematic excess optimism about outcomes. |
| 14 | Deprival-Super Reaction | `deprival-super-reaction` | Overreact to near-loss, threat of loss, or perceived deprivation. |
| 15 | Social-Proof | `social-proof` | Follow what others do, especially under uncertainty or similarity. |
| 16 | Contrast-Misreaction | `contrast-misreaction` | Perceive by relative contrast, not absolute scale; miss slow incremental change. |
| 17 | Stress-Influence | `stress-influence` | Stress amplifies and distorts other psychological tendencies. |
| 18 | Availability-Misweighing | `availability-misweighing` | Overweight vivid, recent, or easily recalled information. |
| 19 | Use-It-or-Lose-It | `use-it-or-lose-it` | Skills and knowledge decay without regular practice. |
| 20 | Drug-Misinfluence | `drug-misinfluence` | Chemical alteration impairs judgment (alcohol, drugs). |
| 21 | Senescence-Misinfluence | `senescence-misinfluence` | Age-related cognitive decline affects judgment. |
| 22 | Authority-Misinfluence | `authority-misinfluence` | Excessive deference to authority figures and their directives. |
| 23 | Twaddle | `twaddle` | Waste time and attention on trivial or irrelevant discourse. |
| 24 | Reason-Respecting | `reason-respecting` | Comply more readily when given reasons — especially *why*. |
| 25 | Lollapalooza | `lollapalooza` | Extreme outcomes when multiple tendencies align toward the same end. |
